🗿

Granite

Type: Igneous (Intrusive)
Hardness: 6-7
Coarse-grained with visible crystals of quartz, feldspar, and mica.

⬛

Basalt

Type: Igneous (Extrusive)
Hardness: 5-6
Fine-grained, dark volcanic rock, often with gas bubbles.

🧱

Limestone

Type: Sedimentary
Hardness: 3-4
Fizzes with acid, often contains fossils, light colored.

🟫

Sandstone

Type: Sedimentary
Hardness: 6-7
Grainy texture, made of sand-sized particles cemented together.

⬜

Marble

Type: Metamorphic
Hardness: 3-4
Recrystallized limestone, often with colorful veins.

💎

Quartz

Type: Mineral
Hardness: 7
Very hard, glassy luster, hexagonal crystals, no cleavage.

🔷

Feldspar

Type: Mineral
Hardness: 6-6.5
Good cleavage, common in igneous rocks, various colors.

✨

Mica

Type: Mineral
Hardness: 2.5-3
Perfect cleavage, splits into thin sheets, shiny.

💧 Acid Test

Carbonate minerals like calcite fizz when exposed to dilute hydrochloric acid, producing CO₂ bubbles.

🌟 Luster Test

Luster describes how light reflects from a mineral's surface: metallic, vitreous (glassy), pearly, silky, or dull.

📏 Cleavage & Fracture

Cleavage is the tendency to break along flat planes. Fracture is irregular breakage.
