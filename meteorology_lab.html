Lab 1: The Water Cycle

The water cycle (hydrologic cycle) describes the continuous movement of water on, above, and below Earth's surface.

25°C

💧 Water Cycle Stages:

1. Evaporation: Water transforms from liquid to vapor due to heat energy

2. Condensation: Water vapor cools and forms clouds

3. Precipitation: Water falls as rain, snow, sleet, or hail

4. Collection: Water gathers in oceans, lakes, rivers, and groundwater

5. Transpiration: Plants release water vapor through leaves

Lab 4: Air Pressure and Weather Systems

Air pressure is the force exerted by the weight of air in the atmosphere. Changes in air pressure create wind and weather patterns.

1013 mb

📊 Pressure System Data:

Current Pressure: 1013 mb
System Type: Normal
Expected Weather: Stable
Wind Direction: Variable

🌪️ Pressure Systems:

High Pressure (Anticyclone): Air sinks, clear skies, calm weather. Winds flow clockwise (Northern Hemisphere).

Low Pressure (Cyclone): Air rises, clouds form, stormy weather. Winds flow counterclockwise (Northern Hemisphere).

Standard Pressure: 1013.25 mb (millibars) or 29.92 inches Hg at sea level
