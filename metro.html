💧 Water Cycle in a Bag

The water cycle is the continuous movement of water on, above, and below Earth's surface. This simple experiment demonstrates evaporation, condensation, and precipitation in action!

Materials Needed:

  • Clear plastic ziplock bag
  • Water (colored with blue food coloring)
  • Permanent marker
  • Tape
  • Sunny window

Procedure:

  1. Draw a simple water cycle diagram on the bag with the marker
  2. Pour about 1/4 cup of water (with blue food coloring) into the bag
  3. Seal the bag tightly, removing as much air as possible
  4. Tape the bag to a sunny window
  5. Observe changes over several hours and days
  6. Notice water droplets forming at the top (condensation)
  7. Watch as droplets fall back down (precipitation)

🌡️ Greenhouse Effect Demonstration

The greenhouse effect is a natural process that warms Earth's surface. This experiment demonstrates how certain gases trap heat, similar to how greenhouse gases work in our atmosphere.

Materials Needed:

  • Two identical glass jars
  • Two thermometers
  • Plastic wrap or glass lid
  • Lamp or sunlight
  • Timer or watch

Procedure:

  1. Place a thermometer in each jar
  2. Cover one jar tightly with plastic wrap or a lid
  3. Leave the other jar open
  4. Place both jars in direct sunlight or under a lamp
  5. Record the starting temperature in both jars
  6. Wait 30 minutes and record temperatures again
  7. Observe which jar gets warmer and discuss why
  8. The covered jar represents Earth's atmosphere trapping heat

☁️ Cloud in a Jar

Clouds form when water vapor in the air cools and condenses around tiny particles. This experiment recreates the cloud formation process in a jar!

Materials Needed:

  • Large glass jar with lid
  • Hot water (boiling)
  • Ice cubes
  • Metal tray or plate
  • Matches or incense stick
  • Black paper (for background)

Procedure:

  1. Pour hot water into the jar (about 2 inches deep)
  2. Place the metal tray with ice cubes on top of the jar
  3. Wait a moment, then remove the tray briefly
  4. Light a match, blow it out, and drop it into the jar
  5. Quickly replace the ice-covered tray on top
  6. Watch as a cloud forms inside the jar!
  7. Place black paper behind the jar for better visibility
  8. Observe: warm air rises, cools, and condenses around smoke particles

🎈 Air Pressure Magic

Air pressure is the force exerted by air molecules. Changes in air pressure drive weather patterns and can create fascinating demonstrations like the classic egg-in-a-bottle experiment!

Materials Needed:

  • Glass bottle with narrow neck
  • Hard-boiled egg (peeled)
  • Paper strip
  • Matches or lighter
  • Adult supervision required

Procedure:

  1. Ensure the egg can sit on the bottle opening but not fall in
  2. Light a small piece of paper and drop it into the bottle
  3. Quickly place the egg on the bottle opening
  4. Watch as the fire consumes oxygen and cools the air inside
  5. Observe the egg being sucked into the bottle!
  6. Explanation: Air pressure outside is greater than inside, pushing the egg in
  7. To remove: Turn bottle upside down and blow hard into it
